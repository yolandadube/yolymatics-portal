📡 Module 1
What Is Light?
☀️
Electromagnetic Radiation
Light is a form of electromagnetic radiation. It travels in a straight line outward from its source in all directions — like rays spreading from the Sun.
🌊
No Medium Needed
Unlike sound, light does not need a medium to travel. It can move through the vacuum of outer space at 300,000 km/s.
🌈
Visible Spectrum
Visible light is just a small portion of the full electromagnetic spectrum. It includes all the colours of the rainbow: red, orange, yellow, green, blue, indigo, and violet.
🔬
Particle & Wave Nature
Light behaves as both a wave and a particle (photon). This dual nature is one of the great discoveries of modern physics.
⚡ Module 2
Electromagnetic vs Matter Waves
⚡ Electromagnetic Waves
💡 Examples: Light, X-rays, Radio waves, Microwaves
🚀 Do NOT need a medium — travel through vacuum
🌍 Travel at the speed of light (c = 3×10⁸ m/s)
📡 Can be reflected, refracted, or absorbed
☀️ Main source on Earth: the Sun
VS
🔊 Matter Waves
🎵 Examples: Sound waves, Water waves, Seismic waves
💨 Require a medium — solid, liquid, or gas
🐢 Travel much slower than light
🪨 Need to collide with particles to travel
🌌 Cannot travel through outer space
🔭 Module 3
Three Key Properties of Light
🪞
Reflection
Light bounces back when it hits a surface. The angle at which it hits equals the angle at which it bounces (angle of incidence = angle of reflection).
🔮
Refraction
Light bends when it passes from one medium to another. The change in speed causes the change in direction — this is why a straw looks bent in water.
🌑
Absorption
Some materials absorb light energy. The energy is converted to heat. Dark objects absorb more light; this is why they feel warmer in sunlight.
🪞 Module 4
Reflection of Light
Law of Reflection: The angle of incidence always equals the angle of reflection. Both angles are measured from the normal — an imaginary line perpendicular to the surface.
Types of Reflection:
• Specular (regular): smooth surfaces like mirrors → clear image
• Diffuse: rough surfaces like walls → scattered light, no clear image
📌 Real-World Example: When the sun's light reaches Earth, some waves bounce back into outer space. This is reflection on a planetary scale!
🧠 Quiz Time — Question 1
A ray of light hits a flat mirror. The angle of incidence is 35°. What is the angle of reflection?
A 55°
B 35°
C 70°
D 90°
✅ Answer: B — 35°
By the Law of Reflection, the angle of incidence always equals the angle of reflection (θᵢ = θᵣ). Since the incidence angle is 35°, the reflected angle is also 35°.
🔮 Module 5
Refraction of Light
Refraction occurs when light passes from one medium into another. The change in speed causes the light ray to bend. The greater the difference in optical density, the more bending occurs.
📌 Example: A straw in a glass of water appears bent at the surface. Light slows down as it moves from air (less dense) into water (more dense), causing it to bend toward the normal.
Snell's Law
n₁ sin θ₁ = n₂ sin θ₂
n = refractive index  ·  θ = angle from normal
🧠 Quiz Time — Question 2
A beam of light moves from air into water. What happens to its speed and direction?
A Speed increases; bends away from normal
B Speed stays the same; no bending
C Speed decreases; bends toward the normal
D Speed decreases; bends away from normal
✅ Answer: C
Water is optically denser than air. Light slows down as it enters water (from ~3×10⁸ m/s to ~2.25×10⁸ m/s) and bends toward the normal. This is refraction.
🌌 Module 6
Light vs Sound in Space
🚀
Rock Concert in Outer Space?
You wouldn't hear a thing! Sound waves are matter waves — they need particles to collide with in order to travel. Outer space is a near-perfect vacuum with almost no particles. So no medium = no sound.

Light, on the other hand, travels freely through space as an electromagnetic wave. That's why we can see stars millions of light-years away — but we will never hear them!
🌟 Light in space:
Travels at 300,000 km/s through the vacuum. Sunlight takes about 8 minutes to reach Earth.
🔇 Sound in space:
Cannot travel. No air molecules = no vibrations to carry the wave. Total silence!
🧠 Quiz Time — Question 3
Which statement BEST explains why you cannot hear sound in outer space but CAN see stars?
A Stars produce light but not sound
B Sound travels faster than light in space
C Light is an EM wave needing no medium; sound is a matter wave requiring particles
D Space absorbs all sound and reflects all light
✅ Answer: C
Electromagnetic waves (light) do not need a medium to propagate — they can travel through the vacuum of space. Sound waves are matter waves that require particles to carry the vibration. Space has no air particles, so sound cannot travel.
✅ Summary
Key Takeaways
1
Light is a form of electromagnetic radiation that travels in straight lines and needs no medium.
2
Reflection: Light bounces off surfaces. Angle of incidence = Angle of reflection (θᵢ = θᵣ).
3
Refraction: Light bends when it passes between media of different densities. Described by Snell's Law.
4
Absorption: Some materials absorb light, converting it to heat energy. Dark objects absorb more light.
5
EM waves vs Matter waves: Light travels through vacuum; sound needs a medium like air, water, or solid.
6
The Sun is Earth's main light source. Its waves are reflected, refracted, and absorbed as they interact with our atmosphere.
