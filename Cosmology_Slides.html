Anchoring Phenomenon
What is Cosmology?

Cosmology is the scientific study of the origin, structure, and evolution of the universe — from its earliest moments to its ultimate fate.

🌌

Ancient Myths → Modern Science

Since ancient times, cultures used mythology to explain the universe's origin. Modern astronomers replaced myths with verifiable evidence from telescopes, spectra, and mathematics.

🔭

How Cosmologists Work

They observe stars, galaxies, and light across billions of light-years. New evidence refines or replaces old theories — this is how science progresses.

Motion of Galaxies
🔍 Focus Question: What evidence suggests the universe is expanding?
Edwin Hubble — Father of Cosmology
👨‍🔬

Edwin Hubble (1929)

Published observations showing that galaxies are moving away from Earth at incredible speeds.

🔭

The Big Discovery

Most astronomers believed the universe = the Milky Way. Hubble showed that distant "star clusters" were actually entire other galaxies — billions of them!

💫

The Revolution

The universe was not static — it was composed of billions of galaxies, most rapidly moving away due to the infinite expansion of space itself.

❌ Old Belief

↓

✅ Hubble's Discovery

Motion of Galaxies
Rapid Redshifts — Evidence of Expansion

Spectrum analyses of distant galaxies showed light much redder than expected — galaxies were moving away from Earth at hundreds of km/s.

🔴

What is Redshift?

When a light source moves away from you, its wavelengths stretch toward the red end of the spectrum. The faster it moves away, the more red-shifted the light.

🌌

The Key Conclusion

Redshift from every direction in the sky confirmed the universe is expanding in all directions — not just galaxies moving through space, but space itself stretching.

Light Spectrum: Stationary vs Moving Away

🔵 Stationary galaxy — normal spectrum

🔴 Receding galaxy — redshifted spectrum

Absorption lines shifted toward red → galaxy moving away!

Speed of receding galaxies: hundreds of km/s

Motion of Galaxies
Hubble's Law & The Hubble Constant

Hubble found that the speed of distant galaxies increases in proportion to their distance from Earth. The farther the galaxy, the faster it recedes.

Hubble's Law

v = H₀ × d

v

velocity of the galaxy

H₀

Hubble constant (today)

d

distance of the galaxy

📅

Age of the Universe

Calculated from the reciprocal of H₀ → between 13–14 billion years

Speed vs Distance — Hubble's Graph

Each dot = a galaxy. The linear relationship confirms: farther = faster

Center of the Universe
🔍 Focus Question: What is the cosmological principle?
The Cosmological Principle

The cosmological principle states that the universe is isotropic and homogeneous — the same in all directions and all locations.

🔄

Isotropic

Looks the same in every direction from our location

🌐

Homogeneous

Same structure and density everywhere in space

🎯

No Special Center

Every location in the universe appears to be its center. The Hubble expansion is smooth and consistent across the entire sky.

Vocabulary
Key Terms — Cosmology

Cosmology

The scientific study of the origin, structure, and evolution of the universe

Big Bang Theory

The universe began from an extremely dense, hot point and has been expanding ever since

Primeval Atom

The original extremely dense point from which the Big Bang originated — atom-sized but containing all matter

Hubble's Law

The speed of a galaxy's recession is proportional to its distance: v = H₀ × d

Hubble Constant

H₀ — the constant of proportionality in Hubble's Law. Its reciprocal gives the age of the universe

Cosmological Principle

The universe is isotropic and homogeneous — uniform in all directions and locations

Isotropic

The universe looks the same in every direction when observed from any point

Homogeneous

The universe has the same structure, density and composition everywhere in space
