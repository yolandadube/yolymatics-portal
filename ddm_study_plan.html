● Deep dive — DDM core theory 6 / 12
What You Must Understand About DDM
The mathematical core — every equation you need to be fluent in
DDM EoS
\(w_{\rm dm}(a) = w_0 + w_a(1-a)\)
Continuity equation
\(\dot\rho_i + 3H(1+w_i)\rho_i = \mathcal{Q}_i\)
Interaction kernel
\(\mathcal{Q} = \xi H \rho_{\rm dm}\) (or other forms)
Friedmann modified
\(H^2 = H_0^2(\Omega_m^{\rm eff}(z) + \Omega_\Lambda)\)
The three DDM frameworks you must know
Framework 1 — Dynamical DM equation of state: DM has \(w_{\rm dm}(z) \neq 0\). Background energy density evolves as \(\rho_{\rm dm} \propto a^{-3(1+w_{\rm dm})}\). The CPL form \(w_{\rm dm}(a) = w_0 + w_a(1-a)\) is the simplest parametrisation. Modifies \(P(k)\) via the transfer function.
Framework 2 — Decaying dark matter (\(\Lambda\)DDM): DM particle decays: DM \(\to\) massless radiation + massive daughter with velocity kick \(v_k\). The decay rate \(\Gamma\) and \(v_k\) are free parameters. Suppresses \(P(k)\) at small scales \(k \gtrsim k_{\rm decay}\), potentially resolving \(\sigma_8\) tension.
Framework 3 — Interacting DE-DM (IDE): Energy exchange between dark sectors: \(\dot\rho_{\rm dm} + 3H\rho_{\rm dm} = +\mathcal{Q}\), \(\dot\rho_{\rm de} + 3H(1+w_{\rm de})\rho_{\rm de} = -\mathcal{Q}\). Common kernels: \(\mathcal{Q} = \xi H\rho_{\rm dm}\) or \(\mathcal{Q} = \xi H\rho_{\rm de}\). Can address coincidence problem and \(H_0\) tension.
What DDM changes observationally
Background expansion: \(H(z)\) is modified — shifts BAO peak position \(r_s(z_{\rm drag})\) and changes \(d_A(z)\). DESI constrains this directly.
Matter power spectrum \(P(k)\): modified transfer function — suppression at \(k \gg k_{\rm eq}\) for decaying DDM; shift in turnover \(k_0\) for interacting IDE. These are the scales Euclid and MegaMapper measure.
Growth factor \(D_+(a)\): the growth equation \(\ddot\delta + 2H\dot\delta - 4\pi G\bar\rho_{\rm dm}^{\rm eff}\delta = 0\) changes — \(f\sigma_8(z)\) in RSD is modified. Measured by DESI full-shape analysis.
Bispectrum \(B(k_1,k_2,k_3)\): tree-level bispectrum kernel \(F_2\) is modified; relativistic corrections in DDM have not been fully computed — this is your research opportunity.

● 17 May — Supervisor meeting prep 11 / 12
What to Say on 17 May
Walk in with a research direction, not just reading notes — impress Prof. Maartens with concrete proposals
Five things to communicate confidently
1. The DDM landscape: "I have studied the three main DDM frameworks — dynamical \(w_{\rm dm}(z)\), decaying DDM, and interacting IDE — and understand how each modifies \(P(k)\) and the growth rate \(f\sigma_8(z)\)."
2. The observational motivation: "DESI DR2 shows \(\sim 3\sigma\) evidence for \(w \neq -1\) in dark energy, and the \(\sigma_8\) tension motivates dynamical dark matter. These two findings together make DDM a well-motivated research direction."
3. The gap you have identified: "The relativistic galaxy bispectrum has not been computed for any DDM model. My existing bispectrum work provides the exact toolkit needed to do this."
4. The turnover extension: "DDM shifts the turnover scale \(k_0\) via its modified thermal history. I can quantify this shift for Euclid and MegaMapper using my existing cobaya pipeline — this is a natural second paper."
5. A specific question for Prof. Maartens: "Which DDM framework do you think is most theoretically motivated for a first paper — decaying DDM or interacting IDE? And should we use the \(1+3\) covariant formalism for the perturbations?"
What to bring on 17 May
A one-page research proposal: title, motivation (DDM + tensions), methodology (your bispectrum formalism + DDM perturbations), expected outputs (2 papers: relativistic DDM bispectrum + turnover in DDM)
A list of 3 candidate DDM papers to discuss — Wang et al. 2025, Bucko et al. 2024, and one on IDE — showing you have engaged with the primary literature
A draft equation sheet: the DDM continuity equations, modified Friedmann equation, and perturbed equations — written out in the \(1+3\) notation Prof. Maartens uses
A timeline: realistic milestones — DDM theory mastery (done by 17 May), first analytical results (3 months), first paper draft (6 months) — showing Prof. Maartens you are organised and self-directed
Key message for the meeting: you are not asking "what should I work on?" — you are presenting a concrete, well-motivated research direction and asking for his expert guidance on which specific framework and formalism to prioritise. That is what a confident PhD researcher does.

● How to execute the plan 12 / 12
Daily Execution Strategy
How to structure each day to cover the material with deep understanding, not just surface reading
Days available
21 days
Recommended daily study
6–8 hrs intensive
Papers per day (avg)
1–2 full papers
Equations to derive daily
At least 3 from scratch
Recommended daily structure
Morning (2–3 hrs) — Theory: work through the day's topic from first principles. Derive every equation on paper. Do not move on until you can reproduce it without notes.
Midday (2–3 hrs) — Reading: read the day's assigned paper(s). Take structured notes: (1) what problem does it solve, (2) what equations are central, (3) what is the key result, (4) what does it leave open.
Afternoon (1–2 hrs) — Connection: explicitly connect what you read to your existing bispectrum and power spectrum work. Write one paragraph: "If I applied this to my JCAP paper, I would need to change X to get Y."
Evening (30 min) — Consolidation: write a brief summary of the day's learning in your own words. Identify one thing you do not yet understand and schedule it for tomorrow morning.
The three rules for intensive mastery
Rule 1 — Derive, do not memorise: every equation in DDM must be something you can derive. If you cannot derive it, you do not understand it yet. Write it out. Every. Time.
Rule 2 — Connect everything to \(P(k)\) and \(B\): every new concept must be immediately mapped to the power spectrum or bispectrum. Ask: "How does this change what Euclid or MegaMapper would measure?"
Rule 3 — Generate questions, not just notes: the measure of your mastery is the quality of the questions you can ask. By Day 21, you should have a list of 10 specific unanswered questions about DDM. These are your research directions.
On 17 May: Prof. Maartens does not expect you to have solved DDM. He expects you to arrive with deep understanding, clear motivation, and a specific direction. That is exactly what this plan delivers. You have 21 days. You are ready for this.
