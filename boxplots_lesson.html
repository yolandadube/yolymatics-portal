📦 Introduction

What Is a Box Plot?


A box plot is a graph that displays the distribution of data grouped into four sections. It shows only the median as a measure of center, and allows easy comparison of data.


Why use it?

  • Shows the full range of data
  • Highlights middle 50% of data
  • Easy to spot skewness
  • Great for comparing two datasets

Key vocabulary

  • Quartile – divides data into 4 equal parts
  • Median – middle value
  • IQR – interquartile range
  • Spread – how far data values vary

📏 Spread & Variability

Distribution & Spread of Data


The spread (variability) of a data set tells us how far apart the values are. We measure it with the Range and the Interquartile Range (IQR).


🔴 High Variability

Q1 and Q3 are far from the median. Large range. Data is spread out.

🟢 Low Variability

Q1 and Q3 are close to the median. Small range. Data is clustered together.


Range = Maximum − Minimum
IQR = Q3 − Q1

💡 Remember

The IQR (the box itself) shows how the middle 50% of the data varies. A wider box → more variability in the middle.

↔️ Shape

Skewness of Data


Skewness occurs when one half of the data points are closer together and the other half are further apart — meaning the data is not symmetric.


◀ Left Skew

Median is closer to Q3. Long left tail. Most data is high.

Left-Skewed (Negative)

↔ Symmetric

Median is in the center of the box. Tails are equal length.

Symmetric (No Skew)

▶ Right Skew

Median is closer to Q1. Long right tail. Most data is low.

Right-Skewed (Positive)

🔑 Key Rule

When a box plot is skewed, one tail is longer and the median is closer to the shorter tail.

✏️ Creating Box Plots

5 Steps to Build a Box Plot


1
Draw a number line with a range that includes all five-number summary values. Make sure your scale is even and well-labelled.
2
Draw a box above the number line, where the left side is at Q1 and the right side is at Q3.
3
Draw a vertical line inside the box at the median (Q2).
4
Draw small vertical lines at the minimum and maximum, and connect them to the box with horizontal whiskers.
5
Label the number line with units and give the box plot a descriptive title.
👨‍🍳 Worked Example

Chef's Recipe Data


A chef recorded the number of recipes created at a restaurant each day for 11 days. The data is shown below:


3 5 7 8 9 10 11 13 14 15 18

Data already sorted from least to greatest.


MinimumSmallest value →3
MaximumLargest value →18
Median (Q2)Middle value (6th of 11) →10
Q1Middle of lower half {3,5,7,8,9} →7
Q3Middle of upper half {11,13,14,15,18} →14

IQR = Q3 − Q1 = 14 − 7 = 7

⚠️ Important

When finding Q1 and Q3, the median is NOT included in either half. Only use the values strictly below or above the median.

🎓 Lesson Summary

What Did We Learn Today?


📦 Box Plot Basics

  • Displays data in 4 equal sections
  • Shows the median as center
  • Built from the Five-Number Summary

🔢 Five-Number Summary

  • Min, Q1, Median, Q3, Max
  • Each section = 25% of data
  • IQR = Q3 − Q1 (middle 50%)

📏 Spread

  • Range = Max − Min
  • IQR = Q3 − Q1
  • Wide box → high variability

↔️ Skewness

  • Left skew: median near Q3
  • Right skew: median near Q1
  • Symmetric: median in center
