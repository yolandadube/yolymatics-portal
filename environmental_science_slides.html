Topic 1 — Air Quality
💨 Air Quality & Air Pollution
Air pollution occurs when harmful substances — called pollutants — are released into the atmosphere, making the air unsafe for living things.

Human activities release pollutants that contaminate the atmosphere

🏭

Industrial

Factories burning fossil fuels release SO₂, CO, and smoke particles

🚗

Transport

Cars and trucks release CO, nitrogen oxides (NOₓ), and unburned hydrocarbons

🌾

Agriculture

Pesticides, burning of crops, and livestock release methane and ammonia

Topic 1 — Ozone Layer
🌐 The Ozone Layer
The ozone layer is a region of Earth's stratosphere (15–35 km above Earth) that contains high concentrations of ozone (O₃). It acts as a natural shield protecting all life from the Sun's harmful ultraviolet (UV) radiation.

The ozone layer absorbs harmful UV-B and UV-C rays, allowing only safe light to reach Earth

🛡️ Why the Ozone Layer is Important

Without the ozone layer, harmful UV radiation would reach Earth's surface — causing skin cancer, cataracts, immune system damage, and harm to marine ecosystems and plant life.

Topic 1 — Ozone Depletion
🕳️ Ozone Layer Depletion

⚠️ What is Ozone Depletion?

Human-made chemicals — especially Chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs) — rise into the stratosphere and break down ozone molecules (O₃), thinning the protective layer. A large hole has formed over Antarctica.

Main Causes

  • CFCs — once used in fridges, air conditioners, aerosol sprays (e.g. deodorants)
  • Halons — used in fire extinguishers
  • Nitrous oxide (N₂O) — from fertilisers and combustion
  • Methyl bromide — used as a pesticide

Effects of Ozone Depletion

  • 🏥 Increased skin cancer and sunburn in humans
  • 👁️ Cataracts and eye damage
  • 🌊 Harm to marine life — UV destroys plankton
  • 🌾 Reduced crop yields — UV damages plant DNA
  • 🌡️ Contributes to climate change

Solutions

  • ✅ The Montreal Protocol (1987) — international agreement to phase out CFCs
  • ✅ Use of CFC-free refrigerants and aerosols
  • ✅ The ozone hole is slowly recovering — expected full recovery by 2065
  • ✅ Reduce use of nitrogen-based fertilisers

🧪 How CFCs Destroy Ozone — Simplified

UV light breaks apart a CFC molecule, releasing a chlorine (Cl) atom. That one chlorine atom can destroy up to 100,000 ozone molecules in a chain reaction:   Cl + O₃ → ClO + O₂   (ozone destroyed!)

Topic 2 — Acid Rain
🌧️ Acid Rain — How It Forms
Acid rain is precipitation (rain, snow, or fog) that is more acidic than normal due to dissolved pollutants from the atmosphere. Normal rain has a pH of about 5.6; acid rain has a pH below 5.6.

SO₂ and NOₓ react with water vapour in clouds to form sulfuric and nitric acids → acid rain falls

🧪 Chemical Reactions

SO₂ + H₂O → H₂SO₃   (sulfurous acid)
2SO₂ + O₂ + 2H₂O → 2H₂SO₄   (sulfuric acid)
4NO₂ + O₂ + 2H₂O → 4HNO₃   (nitric acid)

Topic 2 — Acid Rain Effects
💥 Effects of Acid Rain
🌲

Forests

Dissolves nutrients in soil, weakens trees, strips leaves. Large areas of forests in Europe were destroyed by acid rain

🐟

Aquatic Life

Acidifies lakes and rivers (pH drops below 5) — kills fish, frogs, insects. Entire lake ecosystems can collapse

🏛️

Buildings

Corrodes limestone, marble, and metal. Famous structures like the Taj Mahal and ancient Greek temples are being eroded

🌱

Soil

Leaches away essential minerals (calcium, magnesium) making soil infertile. Releases toxic aluminium into soil

🧑‍🤝‍🧑

Humans

Acid rain in drinking water sources, toxic metal release, and breathing acidic air particles cause respiratory problems

✅ Solutions to Acid Rain

  • Use low-sulfur fuels or cleaner energy sources
  • Fit catalytic converters in cars (reduce NOₓ)
  • Install scrubbers in factory chimneys to remove SO₂
  • Switch to renewable energy (wind, solar, hydro)
  • Liming — adding calcium carbonate to acidified lakes to neutralise the acid
Topic 3 — Greenhouse Effect
🌿 The Greenhouse Effect
The greenhouse effect is a natural process that warms Earth's surface. Without it, Earth would be about −18°C — far too cold for life! The problem is when human activities enhance it too much.

The greenhouse effect: solar energy heats Earth → Earth radiates infrared heat → greenhouse gases trap some heat

Topic 3 — Greenhouse Gases
🧪 Greenhouse Gases — Sources & Effects

🌿 Natural Greenhouse Effect

  • Keeps Earth at an average of +15°C
  • Without it Earth would be −18°C
  • Water vapour (H₂O) is the biggest natural greenhouse gas
  • This is essential for life on Earth

🏭 Enhanced Greenhouse Effect

  • Human activities add extra greenhouse gases
  • More gases → more heat trapped
  • Leads to global warming and climate change
  • CO₂ levels have risen 50% since pre-industrial times

Topic 4 — Global Warming
🌡️ Global Warming
Global warming is the long-term rise in Earth's average surface temperature due to the enhanced greenhouse effect. Earth has warmed by approximately 1.1°C since the pre-industrial era — and is on track for much more.

Global average temperature rise since 1880 — accelerating in recent decades

🔥 Main Causes of Global Warming

🏭 Burning fossil fuels (coal, oil, gas) — biggest cause
🌳 Deforestation — fewer trees to absorb CO₂
🐄 Agriculture — livestock produce large amounts of methane
🚗 Transport — cars, planes, ships burn fuels
🏗️ Industry & manufacturing — cement, steel, chemicals

Topic 4 — Global Warming Effects
🌊 Effects of Global Warming
🧊

Melting Ice

Arctic and Antarctic ice sheets melt, contributing to rising sea levels. Glaciers worldwide are retreating rapidly

🌊

Rising Sea Levels

Melting ice + thermal expansion of seawater → sea levels rise. Low-lying islands and coastal cities at risk of flooding

🌪️

Extreme Weather

More frequent and intense hurricanes, droughts, heatwaves, floods, and wildfires worldwide

🦁

Biodiversity Loss

Habitats destroyed, coral reefs bleached, many species face extinction. Polar bears, penguins, and coral at risk

🌾

Food Security

Droughts and floods reduce crop yields. Changes in seasons disrupt farming. Millions face hunger

🦟

Disease Spread

Warmer temperatures allow disease-carrying insects (e.g. malaria mosquitoes) to spread into new regions

🌍 Impact on South Africa

More frequent droughts in the Western Cape (like Cape Town's "Day Zero" 2018) · Increased flood risk in coastal cities · Heatwaves · Threats to agriculture in the Karoo and Eastern Cape · Bleaching of coral reefs along our coasts

Topic 4 — Solutions
🌱 What Can We Do?

🌍 Global Actions

  • Paris Agreement (2015) — countries agreed to limit warming to 1.5°C
  • Switch to renewable energy — solar, wind, hydro, geothermal
  • Reforestation — plant billions of trees to absorb CO₂
  • Develop electric vehicles and cleaner transport
  • Carbon taxes and emission trading schemes
  • Invest in carbon capture technology

🏠 What YOU Can Do

  • 💡 Use energy-saving light bulbs and appliances
  • 🚶 Walk, cycle, or use public transport
  • ♻️ Reduce, Reuse, Recycle
  • 🌱 Eat less red meat (livestock = methane)
  • 🛁 Save water — water treatment uses energy
  • 🗳️ Vote for and support green policies
  • 📢 Spread awareness — educate others!

Wrap-Up
⭐ Lesson Summary
💨

Air Quality

Pollutants (CO, SO₂, NOₓ, PM, CFCs) from industry, vehicles, and agriculture contaminate the air. Causes health problems and environmental damage.

🌐

Ozone Depletion

CFCs break down ozone (O₃) in the stratosphere. Less ozone → more UV radiation reaches Earth → skin cancer, ecosystem damage. Montreal Protocol is helping recovery.

🌧️

Acid Rain

SO₂ + NOₓ react with water in clouds → H₂SO₄ + HNO₃ (acids). Kills forests, acidifies lakes, corrodes buildings. Reduced by clean fuels and scrubbers.

🌿

Greenhouse Effect

Natural process — keeps Earth warm. Enhanced by human CO₂, CH₄, N₂O emissions. More greenhouse gases → more heat trapped → planet warms up.

🌡️

Global Warming

Earth has warmed ~1.1°C since pre-industrial times. Effects: melting ice, rising seas, extreme weather, biodiversity loss. Paris Agreement aims to limit to 1.5°C.

🌱

Solutions

Renewable energy · Reforestation · Electric vehicles · Reduce, Reuse, Recycle · International agreements · Everyday choices all make a difference!
