● Epoch 1  —  Planck epoch 2 / 15
The Planck Epoch
The quantum gravity wall — the boundary of all known physics
Time
\(0 \to 10^{-43}\ \mathrm{s}\)
Temperature
\(T > 10^{32}\ \mathrm{K}\)
Energy scale
\(E > 10^{19}\ \mathrm{GeV}\)
Density
\(\rho \sim 10^{96}\ \mathrm{kg\,m}^{-3}\)
Forces
All 4 unified
What happens
This is the wall beyond which physics breaks down. General relativity predicts a singularity — infinite density and curvature. Quantum field theory requires a fixed background spacetime, which does not exist here.

Spacetime itself is a quantum foam — topology fluctuates wildly on scales of \(\ell_{\mathrm{P}} \approx 10^{-35}\ \mathrm{m}\). Virtual black holes of mass \(m_{\mathrm{P}}\) form and evaporate every \(t_{\mathrm{P}} \approx 10^{-43}\ \mathrm{s}\).

All four fundamental forces — gravity, electromagnetism, the strong force, and the weak force — are unified into a single superforce. The gravitational coupling \(\alpha_{\rm grav}(E) = (E/E_{\mathrm{P}})^2 = 1\) — gravity is as strong as all other interactions.
Key facts & open questions
Candidate theories: String theory, Loop quantum gravity (LQG), Causal set theory — none yet confirmed experimentally
In LQG: the Big Bang is a Big Bounce — the singularity is replaced by a prior contracting phase at Planck density \(\rho_{\rm Pl} \sim 10^{96}\ \mathrm{kg\,m}^{-3}\)
In the Hartle–Hawking no-boundary proposal: time becomes imaginary near \(t = 0\), removing the singularity — the universe has no beginning in the conventional sense
Trans-Planckian problem: inflationary modes with \(k \gg k_{\mathrm{P}}\) today originated sub-Planckian — their initial state may imprint on \(P(k)\)
Open question: Does the universe have a true beginning at \(t = 0\)? This is the deepest unanswered question in all of physics.

● Epoch 2  —  Grand Unification epoch 3 / 15
Grand Unification Epoch
Gravity separates — the first phase transition in cosmic history
Time
\(10^{-43} \to 10^{-36}\ \mathrm{s}\)
Temperature
\(10^{29} \to 10^{32}\ \mathrm{K}\)
Energy scale
\(10^{15} \to 10^{19}\ \mathrm{GeV}\)
Forces
Gravity + GUT (3 unified)
What happens
As the universe cools past \(T_{\mathrm{P}}\), gravity separates from the other three forces — the first symmetry breaking in cosmic history. Gravity becomes the distinct, weak, long-range force we know today.

The three remaining forces stay unified in a single Grand Unified Theory (GUT) interaction, described by a gauge group such as \(\mathrm{SU}(5)\), \(\mathrm{SO}(10)\), or \(E_6\). The GUT symmetry then breaks: \[\mathcal{G}_{\rm GUT} \;\longrightarrow\; \mathrm{SU}(3)_c \times \mathrm{SU}(2)_L \times \mathrm{U}(1)_Y\] giving us the Standard Model gauge group.

Leptogenesis may generate the baryon asymmetry \(\eta_B = n_B/n_\gamma \approx 6\times10^{-10}\) — responsible for all matter we observe.
Key facts & open questions
GUT theories predict proton decay at rate \(\Gamma_p \propto M_{\rm GUT}^{-4}\) — not yet observed at Super-Kamiokande (limit: \(\tau_p > 1.6\times10^{34}\ \mathrm{yr}\))
GUT symmetry breaking produces magnetic monopoles predicted to be superheavy (\(m_M \sim M_{\rm GUT}/\alpha_{\rm GUT}\)) — inflation dilutes them to undetectable levels
Sakharov conditions for baryogenesis: (1) baryon number violation, (2) C and CP violation, (3) departure from thermal equilibrium — all must hold simultaneously
The three gauge couplings \(\alpha_1\), \(\alpha_2\), \(\alpha_3\) nearly unify at \(E_{\rm GUT} \sim 10^{15}\ \mathrm{GeV}\) in the MSSM (with supersymmetry) but not in the Standard Model alone
Open question: What is the correct GUT gauge group? Why has proton decay not been observed? These are among the deepest unsolved problems in particle physics.

● Epoch 3  —  Inflation 4 / 15
Inflation
Exponential expansion — seeds every structure in the universe
Time
\(\sim 10^{-36} \to 10^{-32}\ \mathrm{s}\)
Expansion
\(\geq 10^{26}\times\)
Scale factor
\(a(t) \propto e^{Ht}\)
Hubble rate
\(H^2 = V(\phi)/3m_{\mathrm{P}}^2\)
Energy scale
\(\sim 10^{15}\text{–}10^{16}\ \mathrm{GeV}\)
What happens
A scalar field — the inflaton \(\phi\) — rolls slowly down its potential \(V(\phi)\), driving de Sitter expansion. The universe doubled every \(\sim 10^{-38}\ \mathrm{s}\), expanding by at least \(10^{26}\) altogether.

Quantum vacuum fluctuations \(\delta\phi_k \sim H/(2\pi)\) were stretched from sub-Planckian scales to macroscopic scales. After crossing the Hubble horizon they became classical density perturbations — the seeds of every galaxy and cluster we observe today, and the turnover scale \(k_0\) in \(P(k)\) that you are detecting.

Reheating ends inflation: the inflaton decays into Standard Model particles, restoring the hot thermal bath at temperature \(T_{\rm reh}\).
Observational imprints
Primordial power spectrum: \(\mathcal{P}_{\mathcal{R}}(k) = A_s(k/k_*)^{n_s-1}\) with \(n_s \approx 0.965\) (Planck 2018) — nearly scale-invariant
Tensor-to-scalar ratio: \(r = \mathcal{P}_T / \mathcal{P}_{\mathcal{R}}\) constrains inflation energy scale via \(V^{1/4} = 1.06\times10^{16}(r/0.01)^{1/4}\ \mathrm{GeV}\). Current limit: \(r < 0.036\)
Inflation solves the flatness problem (\(\Omega \approx 1\)), horizon problem (CMB uniform to \(10^{-5}\)), and monopole problem
The turnover scale \(k_0 \sim 0.01\text{–}0.02\ h\,\mathrm{Mpc}^{-1}\) you detect with Euclid and MegaMapper traces directly to the horizon scale at the end of inflation
Open question: What is the inflaton \(\phi\)? Future CMB \(B\)-mode experiments (LiteBIRD, CMB-S4) will measure \(r\) to \(\pm 0.001\) precision.

● Epoch 4  —  Electroweak epoch 5 / 15
Electroweak Epoch
Reheating, the quark-gluon plasma, and the Higgs mechanism
Time
\(10^{-32} \to 10^{-12}\ \mathrm{s}\)
Temperature
\(10^{15} \to 10^{28}\ \mathrm{K}\)
Energy scale
\(100\ \mathrm{GeV} \to 10^{15}\ \mathrm{GeV}\)
State of matter
Quark-gluon plasma (QGP)
What happens
After inflation, the inflaton decays and reheats the universe, filling it with a hot plasma of quarks, leptons, and gauge bosons in thermal equilibrium. The strong force separates at \(\sim 10^{15}\ \mathrm{GeV}\).

At the electroweak phase transition (\(T_{\rm EW} \approx 160\ \mathrm{GeV} \approx 10^{15}\ \mathrm{K}\)), the Higgs field acquires its vacuum expectation value \(\langle\phi\rangle = v \approx 246\ \mathrm{GeV}\), breaking electroweak symmetry: \[\mathrm{SU}(2)_L \times \mathrm{U}(1)_Y \;\longrightarrow\; \mathrm{U}(1)_{\rm em}\] giving mass \(m_W \approx 80\ \mathrm{GeV}\) to \(W^\pm\) and \(m_Z \approx 91\ \mathrm{GeV}\) to \(Z^0\), while the photon remains massless.
Key facts & open questions
The QGP — where quarks and gluons move freely — was recreated at RHIC and the LHC ALICE experiment at \(T \sim 2\times10^{12}\ \mathrm{K}\)
The Higgs boson (\(m_H \approx 125.1\ \mathrm{GeV}\)) discovered at the LHC in 2012 confirms the Higgs mechanism — the last piece of the Standard Model
Lattice QCD shows the EW transition at \(m_H = 125\ \mathrm{GeV}\) is a smooth crossover, not first-order — this rules out electroweak baryogenesis
All Standard Model particles are in thermal equilibrium; matter–antimatter ratio is \(\sim 1:1\) with tiny excess \(\eta_B \approx 6\times10^{-10}\) surviving from leptogenesis
Open question: If the EW transition is a crossover, how was the baryon asymmetry generated? This remains one of the deepest unsolved problems in particle cosmology.

● Epoch 5  —  QCD transition & BBN 6 / 15
QCD Transition & Big Bang Nucleosynthesis
Quarks confine into protons and neutrons; the first nuclei form
QCD transition
\(t \sim 10^{-6}\ \mathrm{s},\ T \sim 2\times10^{12}\ \mathrm{K}\)
BBN window
\(t \approx 1\ \mathrm{s} \to 3\ \mathrm{min}\)
\(^4\mathrm{He}\) mass fraction
\(Y_p \approx 0.247\)
\(\mathrm{D/H}\) ratio
\(\approx 2.5\times10^{-5}\)
What happens
At \(T \approx 2\times10^{12}\ \mathrm{K}\) (\(\sim 200\ \mathrm{MeV}\)), the strong coupling grows and quarks and gluons bind into protons and neutrons. The quark-gluon plasma gives way to a hadron gas — this is the QCD confinement transition.

At \(t \approx 1\ \mathrm{s}\), neutrinos decouple and the neutron-to-proton ratio freezes at \(n/p \approx 1/7\). Between \(t \approx 1\ \mathrm{s}\) and \(t \approx 3\ \mathrm{min}\), Big Bang Nucleosynthesis (BBN) fuses protons and neutrons into: \[{}^2\mathrm{H},\quad {}^3\mathrm{He},\quad {}^4\mathrm{He},\quad {}^7\mathrm{Li}\] The predicted abundances match observations — one of the greatest triumphs of the Standard Model of cosmology.
BBN predictions vs observations
Helium-4: \(Y_p^{\rm BBN} \approx 0.247\) — matches measurements in metal-poor HII regions and old stars to \(\sim 1\%\) precision
Deuterium: \((\mathrm{D/H})_{\rm BBN} \approx 2.5\times10^{-5}\) — measured in quasar absorption spectra; directly constrains \(\Omega_b h^2\)
BBN as a probe: number of neutrino species \(N_\nu\) and the baryon density \(\Omega_b h^2\) are both constrained by comparing predicted to observed abundances
Lithium problem: BBN predicts \(({}^7\mathrm{Li/H}) \approx 5\times10^{-10}\), but metal-poor halo stars (the Spite plateau) show \(\sim 3\times\) less — still unresolved
Open question: The lithium-7 problem — new physics, stellar depletion, or systematic error? Decades of effort have not resolved it.

● Epoch 6  —  Lepton epoch 7 / 15
Lepton Epoch
Neutrino decoupling, \(e^\pm\) annihilation, and the Cosmic Neutrino Background
Time
\(\sim 1 \to 10\ \mathrm{s}\)
Temperature
\(5\times10^9 \to 10^{10}\ \mathrm{K}\)
Energy scale
\(\sim 0.5 \to 1\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
Content
\(e^\pm\), \(\nu\), \(\bar\nu\), \(\gamma\), \(p\), \(n\)
What happens
At \(T \sim 10^{10}\ \mathrm{K}\) (\(\sim 1\ \mathrm{MeV}\)), neutrinos decouple from the thermal bath — their mean free path exceeds the Hubble radius. They free-stream forever as the Cosmic Neutrino Background (C\(\nu\)B).

Shortly after, as \(T\) drops below \(m_e c^2 \approx 0.511\ \mathrm{MeV}\), electrons and positrons annihilate: \(e^+ + e^- \to \gamma + \gamma\). This reheats the photon gas but not the already-decoupled neutrinos, creating a permanent temperature split: \[T_\gamma = \left(\frac{11}{4}\right)^{1/3} T_\nu \approx 1.401\, T_\nu\] The photon temperature is boosted relative to \(T_\nu\) by the factor \((11/4)^{1/3}\), a result that follows from entropy conservation.
Key facts & open questions
C\(\nu\)B temperature today: \(T_\nu = (4/11)^{1/3} T_0 \approx 1.945\ \mathrm{K}\) — the universe is permeated by \(\sim 336\ \mathrm{cm}^{-3}\) relic neutrinos
Effective number of relativistic species: \(N_{\rm eff} = 3.044\) in the SM; CMB constraint: \(N_{\rm eff} = 2.99\pm0.17\) (Planck 2018) — future CMB-S4 target: \(\pm 0.02\)
The PTOLEMY experiment aims to detect C\(\nu\)B via \(\bar\nu_e + {}^3\mathrm{H} \to {}^3\mathrm{He} + e^-\) — the first direct neutrino background detection would be a landmark
After \(e^\pm\) annihilation: the universe is a plasma of photons, protons, neutrons, and electrons, about to enter the radiation-dominated era
Open question: Neutrino mass \(\sum m_\nu < 0.12\ \mathrm{eV}\) (Planck + BAO). Absolute mass scale and mass ordering (normal vs inverted hierarchy) remain unknown.

● Epoch 7  —  Photon domination 8 / 15
Photon Domination Era
Radiation rules — acoustic oscillations build up — the turnover scale is set
Time
\(10\ \mathrm{s} \to 47{,}000\ \mathrm{yr}\)
Scale factor
\(a(t) \propto t^{1/2}\)
Equation of state
\(w = 1/3\)
Energy density
\(\rho_r \propto a^{-4}\)
Ends at
\(z_{\rm eq} \approx 3400\)
What happens
Radiation (photons + relativistic neutrinos) dominates the energy budget. The universe is an opaque photon-baryon plasma — photons and baryons tightly coupled via Thomson scattering.

Pressure waves travel through this plasma at the sound speed: \[c_s = \frac{c}{\sqrt{3\bigl(1 + 3\rho_b/4\rho_\gamma\bigr)}}\] These are the baryon acoustic oscillations (BAO) — they create the acoustic peaks in \(C_\ell\) and the BAO feature in \(P(k)\). This era ends at matter-radiation equality \(z_{\rm eq} \approx 3400\) when \(\rho_m = \rho_r\). The comoving Hubble scale at equality sets the turnover scale \(k_0 \approx k_{\rm eq} = a_{\rm eq} H_{\rm eq}\) in your research.
Key facts — direct connection to your research
Turnover scale: \(k_0 \approx k_{\rm eq}\) in \(P(k)\) is set here — the shape of the power spectrum around \(k_0 \sim 0.01\text{–}0.02\ h\,\mathrm{Mpc}^{-1}\) encodes \(\Omega_m h\) and \(H_0\)
Meszaros effect: dark matter perturbations are suppressed during radiation domination — growth \(\delta_m \propto \ln a\) instead of \(\propto a\) — this imprints on \(P(k)\) for \(k \gg k_{\rm eq}\)
BAO sound horizon: \(r_s = \int_0^{t_{\rm drag}} c_s\,dt/a \approx 147\ \mathrm{Mpc}\) — a standard ruler used to measure \(H_0\) and \(\Omega_m\)
Transfer function \(T(k)\): encodes all radiation-era suppression; \(P(k) = A_s k^{n_s} T^2(k)\) connects the primordial spectrum to what surveys measure
Connection to your research: The shape of \(P(k)\) around \(k_0\) — which you detect in the power spectrum and bispectrum — is entirely determined by the physics of this epoch.

● Epoch 8  —  Matter domination 9 / 15
Matter Domination Era
Structure grows — dark matter halos, galaxies, and clusters assemble
Redshift range
\(z_{\rm eq} \approx 3400 \to z \approx 0.4\)
Scale factor
\(a(t) \propto t^{2/3}\)
Equation of state
\(w = 0\)
Growth of perturbations
\(\delta_m \propto a \propto (1+z)^{-1}\)
What happens
Matter (dark matter + baryons) dominates the energy budget. The scale factor grows as \(a \propto t^{2/3}\) and \(\rho_m \propto a^{-3}\). Density perturbations grow linearly as \(\delta_m \propto a\) — the primordial seeds from inflation amplify into the large-scale structure of the universe.

The linear growth equation is: \[\ddot\delta + 2H\dot\delta - 4\pi G\bar\rho\,\delta = 0\] with growing solution \(\delta \propto D_+(a)\), the growth factor. Dark matter halos collapse first (hierarchical clustering), then baryonic gas falls into these potential wells, cools, and forms galaxies. The cosmic web — sheets, filaments, voids, and nodes — is the large-scale imprint of \(P(k)\) amplified by gravitational instability.
Key facts & open questions
The two-point correlation function \(\xi(r)\) and power spectrum \(P(k)\) are statistical descriptions of the matter density field grown during this era — directly what Euclid and MegaMapper measure
Recombination (\(z \approx 1100\)) occurs within this era — the CMB is released, and baryons fall freely into dark matter potential wells for the first time
Galaxy bias: \(\delta_g = b\,\delta_m\) — galaxies are biased tracers of the dark matter field; scale-dependent and relativistic bias corrections are crucial for precision surveys
Halo mass function \(n(M,z)\) — the number density of dark matter halos per mass per volume — is predicted by the Press-Schechter formalism and its extensions
Open question: The \(\sigma_8\) tension — CMB-predicted amplitude of matter fluctuations is \(2\text{–}3\sigma\) above weak-lensing surveys. New physics or unaccounted systematics?

● Epoch 9  —  Recombination & last scattering 10 / 15
Recombination & the CMB
The universe becomes transparent — the Cosmic Microwave Background is born
Time
\(\approx 380{,}000\ \mathrm{yr}\)
Redshift
\(z \approx 1100\)
Temperature then
\(\approx 3000\ \mathrm{K}\)
CMB today
\(T_0 = 2.7255\ \mathrm{K}\)
Anisotropy
\(\Delta T/T \sim 10^{-5}\)
What happens
When \(T \approx 3000\ \mathrm{K}\), protons and electrons combine to form neutral hydrogen. The photon mean free path diverges — the universe becomes transparent. The photons that last-scatter are detected today as the Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB) — a perfect blackbody at \(T_0 = 2.7255\ \mathrm{K}\).

The tiny temperature fluctuations \(\Delta T/T \sim 10^{-5}\) are the imprint of primordial density perturbations, processed by acoustic oscillations in the photon-baryon fluid. The angular power spectrum \(C_\ell\) is the Rosetta Stone of cosmology — it encodes: \[C_\ell \;\supset\; \Omega_b h^2,\; \Omega_c h^2,\; H_0,\; n_s,\; A_s,\; \tau\]
What the CMB encodes
\(\Omega_b h^2\) — baryon density: from the ratio of odd to even acoustic peaks in \(C_\ell\)
\(\Omega_c h^2\) — dark matter density: from peak amplitude suppression (more DM reduces the baryon-photon driving)
\(H_0\) — Hubble constant: from the angular scale of the first peak \(\theta_* = r_s(z_*)/D_A(z_*)\) with \(\ell_1 \approx 220\)
\(P(k) = A_s k^{n_s} T^2(k)\) — the matter power spectrum you measure is connected to \(C_\ell\) via the transfer function \(T(k)\)
Open question: Hubble tension — \(H_0 = 67.4\ \mathrm{km\,s^{-1}Mpc^{-1}}\) (CMB) vs \(73\ \mathrm{km\,s^{-1}Mpc^{-1}}\) (distance ladder) — a \(\sim 5\sigma\) discrepancy possibly indicating new physics beyond \(\Lambda\)CDM.

● Epoch 10  —  The Cosmic Dark Ages 11 / 15
The Cosmic Dark Ages
No stars, no light — only dark matter and neutral hydrogen assembling in silence
Time span
\(380{,}000\ \mathrm{yr} \to \sim 150\ \mathrm{Myr}\)
Redshift range
\(z \approx 1100 \to z \approx 30\)
Universe content
Neutral H, He, dark matter
Light sources
None — completely dark
What happens
After recombination, the universe enters a period of complete darkness. No stars or galaxies exist. The universe is filled with neutral hydrogen and helium, slowly cooling, while dark matter halos quietly assemble through gravitational collapse.

The gas temperature evolves as \(T_{\rm gas} \propto (1+z)^2\) (adiabatic cooling) until it decouples from the CMB at \(z \sim 200\), then as \(T_{\rm gas} \propto (1+z)^2\). Dark matter halos grow hierarchically — small halos form first then merge. By \(z \sim 30\), the first halos of mass \(\sim 10^5\text{–}10^6\ M_\odot\) (minihalos) have formed, ready to host the first stars.

The only radiation is the redshifting CMB and the C\(\nu\)B. The universe is opaque to visible light but transparent to the 21-cm hydrogen spin-flip line.
Key facts & open questions
The 21-cm line (\(\lambda = 21.1\ \mathrm{cm}\), \(\nu = 1420\ \mathrm{MHz}\)) from neutral hydrogen spin-flip transition is the primary probe of the Dark Ages — the Square Kilometre Array (SKA) will map it
The global 21-cm signal is observable as an absorption feature against the CMB: \(\delta T_b \propto (1 - T_{\rm CMB}/T_S)\) where \(T_S\) is the spin temperature
EDGES (2018) reported an anomalous absorption at \(z \sim 17\) (\(\delta T_b \approx -500\ \mathrm{mK}\)) — \(\sim 2\times\) deeper than standard predictions; possibly indicating dark matter-baryon interactions
First stars (Population III) end the Dark Ages at \(z \sim 20\text{–}30\) by emitting UV radiation that begins to ionise surrounding gas
Open question: The EDGES anomaly remains controversial — the REACH experiment is designed to provide a definitive answer. New physics in the dark sector could explain it.

● Epoch 11  —  Cosmic reionisation 12 / 15
Cosmic Reionisation
The first stars and quasars ionise the universe — cosmic dawn begins
Time span
\(\sim 150\ \mathrm{Myr} \to 1\ \mathrm{Gyr}\)
Redshift range
\(z \approx 6 \to 30\)
First stars (Pop III)
\(z \sim 20\text{–}30\)
Optical depth
\(\tau = 0.054 \pm 0.007\)
What happens
The first stars — Population III (Pop III) stars — are massive (\(\sim 100\text{–}1000\ M_\odot\)), extremely hot (\(T_{\rm eff} \sim 10^5\ \mathrm{K}\)), and completely metal-free. Their intense UV radiation creates expanding ionised bubbles (HII regions) in the surrounding neutral hydrogen.

As more stars and early galaxies form, these bubbles grow and overlap. Early quasars (AGN) also contribute significantly to ionising the intergalactic medium (IGM). By \(z \approx 6\) (\(t \approx 1\ \mathrm{Gyr}\)), reionisation is essentially complete — the IGM is fully ionised.

The optical depth to reionisation \(\tau = 0.054\) is measured from the large-scale CMB \(E\)-mode polarisation — it damps small-scale temperature fluctuations by \(e^{-2\tau}\).
Key facts & open questions
The Gunn-Peterson trough — complete Ly\(\alpha\) absorption in quasar spectra at \(z > 6\) — demonstrates that the IGM was neutral before \(z \approx 6\) (confirmed by SDSS)
JWST is detecting galaxies at \(z > 12\) — directly observing the first sources driving reionisation. Early results suggest reionisation began at \(z \sim 20\text{–}25\)
Pop III stars end as core-collapse supernovae or direct collapse black holes — seeding the universe with the first metals and the first seeds of supermassive black holes
The 21-cm signal from reionisation is the primary target of the SKA and HERA radio telescopes — a new observational window on cosmic dawn
Open question: What drove reionisation — stars, quasars, or both? What were the masses of Pop III stars? JWST and SKA are answering these questions in real time.

● Epoch 12  —  Dark energy domination 13 / 15
Dark Energy Domination
Accelerated expansion — the cosmological constant \(\Lambda\) takes over
Transition redshift
\(z_{\rm acc} \approx 0.4\)
Equation of state
\(w = p/\rho \approx -1\)
\(\Omega_\Lambda\) today
\(\approx 0.685\)
Scale factor
\(a(t) \propto e^{H_0\sqrt{\Omega_\Lambda}\,t}\)
What happens
At \(z \approx 0.4\), the dark energy density \(\rho_\Lambda = \Lambda c^2/8\pi G\) exceeds the matter density \(\rho_m \propto a^{-3}\). The expansion of the universe begins to accelerate.

Dark energy has negative pressure \(p_\Lambda = w\rho_\Lambda c^2\) with \(w \approx -1\), acting as a repulsive force on cosmic scales. The cosmological constant \(\Lambda\) (or a dynamical quintessence field) drives this. The modified Friedmann equation: \[H^2 = H_0^2\bigl(\Omega_m(1+z)^3 + \Omega_\Lambda\bigr)\] The discovery of accelerated expansion (Riess, Perlmutter & Schmidt 1998, Nobel Prize 2011) via Type Ia supernovae was one of the most surprising results in the history of cosmology.
Key facts & open questions
Cosmological constant problem: QFT predicts \(\rho_{\rm vac} \sim m_{\mathrm{P}}^4 \sim 10^{96}\ \mathrm{kg\,m^{-3}}\), but observed \(\rho_\Lambda \sim 10^{-27}\ \mathrm{kg\,m^{-3}}\) — a discrepancy of \(\sim 123\) orders of magnitude
Dark energy suppresses structure growth: the growth factor \(D_+(a)\) grows more slowly than \(a\) in \(\Lambda\)CDM for \(z \lesssim 1\), measurable via the growth rate \(f = d\ln D_+/d\ln a \approx \Omega_m(z)^{0.55}\)
DESI 2024 results hint \(w\) may evolve: \(w_0 = -0.73\), \(w_a = -1.05\) — if confirmed at \(\sim 3\sigma\), this rules out \(\Lambda\) and points to dynamical dark energy (quintessence)
Euclid (launched 2023) will measure \(w_0\) and \(w_a\) to \(\sim 1\%\) using weak lensing + galaxy clustering + \(P(k)\) measurements — your research directly contributes to this
Open question: What is dark energy? Is \(w = -1\) exactly (cosmological constant) or does it evolve? The answer will define the next era of cosmology.

Composition of the universe today
Dark energy (\(\Lambda\)) — 68.5%: drives accelerated expansion; nature completely unknown; worst fine-tuning problem in physics
Dark matter — 26.6%: gravitationally interacts, forms halos and the cosmic web; particle identity unknown — WIMPs, axions, primordial black holes are candidates
Baryonic matter (atoms) — 4.9%: stars, gas, dust, planets, us — everything we can directly observe makes up less than 5% of the universe
