Part 1 — What is Energy?
⚡ What is Energy?
Energy is the ability to do work or cause change. Anything that moves, heats up, lights up, or changes shape requires energy to make it happen.

Key Facts About Energy

  • Energy is measured in Joules (J)
  • Energy cannot be created or destroyed — only transferred or transformed
  • This is called the Law of Conservation of Energy
  • Energy exists in many different forms

Energy in Daily Life

  • 🔆 Sound from a speaker
  • ☀️ Sunlight warming your skin
  • 🔋 A torch lighting up a room
  • ⚡ Eskom electricity powering your home
  • 🏈 A ball rolling down a hill
Part 1 — Energy Concept
💡 Energy = Ability to Do Work
In science, work is done when a force moves an object over a distance. Energy is what makes that possible.
💪

Mechanical Work

Pushing a trolley at Pick n Pay or lifting a school bag uses energy to do mechanical work.

🔥

Heat Work

A gas stove uses chemical energy to produce heat that cooks your food.

⚡

Electrical Work

Electricity from a battery or plug does work to power your phone or kettle.

🎉

Light Work

The Sun radiates light energy that travels 150 million km to reach Earth and power life.

Part 2 — Main Types
Two Main Types of Energy
All forms of energy belong to one of two main families: Potential Energy (stored) or Kinetic Energy (moving).
A stationary rock on a cliff has potential energy. The moment it falls, that energy becomes kinetic energy.
Part 3 — Kinetic Energy
Kinetic Energy — Energy of Motion
Any object that is moving has kinetic energy. The faster it moves and the greater its mass, the more kinetic energy it has.
KE = ½ × mass × speed²
For Grade 9 you need to understand the concept — the more mass and the faster the speed, the greater the kinetic energy.
🚗

Car on a Highway

A heavy truck moving at 120 km/h has far more kinetic energy than a bicycle at the same speed.

🏈

Rolling Ball

A cricket ball bowled at 140 km/h carries a large amount of kinetic energy towards the batter.

🌊

Ocean Waves

Water molecules moving in ocean waves at places like Muizenberg carry kinetic energy that surfers use.

Part 3 — Forms of Kinetic Energy
Forms of Kinetic Energy
Kinetic energy appears in five main forms depending on how the object or particles are moving.
🆕 Form Description Real-Life Example
Mechanical Energy of large moving objects A skateboard rolling down a ramp; a minibus taxi driving
Thermal (Heat) Energy of fast-moving particles inside a substance Boiling water in a kettle; the warmth of the Sun on your skin
Radiant (Light) Energy carried by electromagnetic waves Sunlight; X-rays at a hospital; your phone screen glowing
Sound Energy from vibrating particles that travel as waves Music from a Bluetooth speaker; thunder after lightning
Electrical Energy from moving electric charges (electrons) Current flowing through wires; charging a cell phone

Part 4 — Potential Energy
Potential Energy — Stored Energy
Potential energy is stored energy that an object has because of its position, shape, or the arrangement of its particles. It is ready to be released as kinetic energy.

Key Idea

  • Not moving yet — but has the potential to do work
  • Stored because of position (height above ground)
  • Stored because of condition (stretched, compressed, chemical bonds)
  • Released when position or condition changes

Everyday Examples

  • 👨 A diver standing at the top of a diving board
  • 🌶 A drawn bow & arrow before release
  • ⛽ Water held behind the Gariep Dam wall
  • 🔋 A battery in a switched-off torch
  • 🍎 The chemical energy stored in your breakfast
Part 4 — Forms of Potential Energy
Forms of Potential Energy
Potential energy is stored in five main ways, depending on what is holding the energy in place.
🆕 Form What Stores the Energy? South African Example
Gravitational Height above the ground and mass of the object Water stored high in the Gariep Dam; a cable car at the top of Table Mountain
Elastic Stretched or compressed shape of an object A stretched rubber band; a compressed spring in a car shock absorber
Chemical Bonds between atoms and molecules Petrol in a car; wood in a braai fire; food you eat for energy
Electrical (Static) Separated electric charges in an electric field Storm clouds before lightning; a charged capacitor in a camera flash
Nuclear Forces holding particles together in an atom's nucleus Koeberg Nuclear Power Station near Cape Town uses nuclear energy

Part 5 — Energy Transformations
🔄 Energy Transformations
An energy transformation happens when energy changes from one form to another. Energy is never lost — it is just converted into a different form.

Key Rules

  • Energy is always conserved (Law of Conservation of Energy)
  • Some energy is often lost as heat (thermal energy)
  • We trace transformations using an energy chain
  • Arrows (→) in a chain show the direction of change

Quick Examples

  • ☀️ Sunlight → Chemical (photosynthesis in plants)
  • 🍎 Chemical (food) → Kinetic (running) + Heat
  • 🔋 Chemical (battery) → Electrical → Light + Heat
  • ⛽ Gravitational → Kinetic → Electrical (dam & turbine)
Part 5 — Energy Chains
⚡ Everyday Energy Chains
Situation Energy Chain
🍎 Eating food & running Chemical → Kinetic (muscles) + Thermal (body heat)
🔋 Battery-powered torch Chemical → Electrical → Light + Thermal
☀️ Plant growing in sunlight Radiant (Sun) → Chemical (photosynthesis in leaves)
🏊 Diver jumping off a board Chemical (muscles) → Gravitational Potential → Kinetic (falling) → Sound + Thermal (splash)
🚘 Car driving on N1 highway Chemical (petrol) → Thermal (combustion) → Mechanical (wheels) + Sound + Heat
