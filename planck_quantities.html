Planck Length
\(\ell_{\mathrm{P}}\)
Formula
\(\displaystyle \ell_{\mathrm{P}} \;=\; \sqrt{\frac{\hbar G}{c^{3}}}\)
Value
\(\approx 1.616 \times 10^{-35}\ \mathrm{m}\)
Compared to proton
\(\approx 10^{-20} \times r_{\mathrm{proton}}\)
The Planck length is the scale at which spacetime itself breaks down. To probe smaller distances, QM demands higher photon energy via \(\lambda = \hbar/p\). But GR says that photon then collapses into a black hole with \(r_{\mathrm{s}} = 2GM/c^2\). The Planck length is where these balance — the Schwarzschild radius equals the de Broglie wavelength. No physical probe can resolve smaller distances.
vs proton radius
A proton is \(\sim 10^{20}\ \ell_{\mathrm{P}}\) across
Scale analogy
Proton : observable universe = \(\ell_{\mathrm{P}}\) : proton
String theory
String length \(\ell_s \sim \ell_{\mathrm{P}}\); extra dims compactify here
Loop quantum gravity
Min. area eigenvalue: \(\Delta A \sim 8\pi\gamma\, \ell_{\mathrm{P}}^{2}\)
Open question: Is there a minimum length in nature? This is experimentally untestable with any foreseeable technology.

Planck Mass
\(m_{\mathrm{P}}\)
Formula
\(\displaystyle m_{\mathrm{P}} \;=\; \sqrt{\frac{\hbar c}{G}}\)
Value
\(\approx 2.176 \times 10^{-8}\ \mathrm{kg}\)
In particle physics units
\(\approx 1.22 \times 10^{19}\ \mathrm{GeV}/c^2\)
The Planck mass is the mass of the smallest possible black hole, whose Schwarzschild radius \(r_{\mathrm{s}} = 2Gm_{\mathrm{P}}/c^{2}\) equals \(\ell_{\mathrm{P}}\). Paradoxically it is not tiny — about 22 micrograms, the mass of a flea egg. This reflects how extraordinarily weak gravity is. In particle physics, \(m_{\mathrm{P}}\) sets the scale of the hierarchy problem: why is \(m_H \approx 125\ \mathrm{GeV}/c^2\) some \(10^{17}\) times smaller than \(m_{\mathrm{P}}\)?
vs proton mass
\(m_{\mathrm{P}} \approx 10^{19}\, m_p\) — 19 orders heavier
Hawking evaporation
Planck-mass black hole evaporates in \(\sim t_{\mathrm{P}}\)
Hierarchy problem
\(m_H / m_{\mathrm{P}} \approx 10^{-17}\) — extreme fine-tuning
GUT scale gap
Grand unification at \(\sim 10^{15}\ \mathrm{GeV}\) is \(10^4\times\) below \(m_{\mathrm{P}}c^2\)
Open question: Supersymmetry, large extra dimensions, and Randall–Sundrum warped geometry all propose solutions to the hierarchy problem. None has been experimentally confirmed at the LHC.

Planck Energy
\(E_{\mathrm{P}}\)
Formula
\(\displaystyle E_{\mathrm{P}} \;=\; m_{\mathrm{P}}\,c^{2} \;=\; \sqrt{\frac{\hbar c^{5}}{G}}\)
Value
\(\approx 1.956 \times 10^{9}\ \mathrm{J}\)
In particle physics units
\(\approx 1.22 \times 10^{19}\ \mathrm{GeV}\)
The gravitational coupling at energy \(E\) is \(\alpha_{\rm grav}(E) = (E/E_{\mathrm{P}})^{2}\), which equals unity at \(E = E_{\mathrm{P}}\) — gravity becomes as strong as all other forces. Above this energy, perturbative quantum gravity is undefined. The LHC reaches \(\sim 13\ \mathrm{TeV}\). The Planck energy is \(10^{15}\times\) higher — beyond any conceivable accelerator.
LHC at 13 TeV
\(10^{15}\times\) below \(E_{\mathrm{P}}\)

GUT scale
\(\sim 10^{15}\ \mathrm{GeV}\) — \(10^4\times\) below \(E_{\mathrm{P}}\)
Macroscopic equivalent
\(E_{\mathrm{P}} \approx\) kinetic energy of a flying mosquito
Open question: Trans-Planckian physics may leave imprints on the primordial power spectrum \(P(k)\). Future surveys (Euclid, MegaMapper) may detect such signatures in \(P(k)\) and \(B(k_1,k_2,k_3)\).

Planck Temperature
\(T_{\mathrm{P}}\)
Formula
\(\displaystyle T_{\mathrm{P}} \;=\; \frac{E_{\mathrm{P}}}{k_{\!B}} \;=\; \sqrt{\frac{\hbar c^{5}}{G\,k_{\!B}^{2}}}\)
Value
\(\approx 1.417 \times 10^{32}\ \mathrm{K}\)
Relation
\(k_{\!B}\,T_{\mathrm{P}} = E_{\mathrm{P}}\)
The highest temperature with any physical meaning in current theory. When \(k_{\!B}T\) reaches \(E_{\mathrm{P}}\), gravity becomes order-unity strong and quantum field theory in curved spacetime breaks down. The universe cooled from \(T_{\mathrm{P}}\) to today's CMB temperature \(T_0 = 2.725\ \mathrm{K}\) — a drop by a factor of \(\sim 5 \times 10^{31}\), driven by cosmic expansion \(T \propto 1/a\).
Sun's core
\(T_\odot \approx 1.5\times10^7\ \mathrm{K}\) — \(10^{25}\times\) below \(T_{\mathrm{P}}\)
CMB today
\(T_0 = 2.725\ \mathrm{K}\) — \(10^{32}\times\) below \(T_{\mathrm{P}}\)
Electroweak transition
\(T_{\rm EW} \approx 10^{15}\ \mathrm{K}\) — \(10^{17}\times\) below \(T_{\mathrm{P}}\)
QCD transition
Quark–hadron crossover: \(\sim 2\times10^{12}\ \mathrm{K}\)
Open question: In string theory, the Hagedorn temperature \(T_{\rm H}\) may set an effective maximum. Its precise relation to \(T_{\mathrm{P}}\) is model-dependent.

Planck Time
\(t_{\mathrm{P}}\)
Formula
\(\displaystyle t_{\mathrm{P}} \;=\; \frac{\ell_{\mathrm{P}}}{c} \;=\; \sqrt{\frac{\hbar G}{c^{5}}}\)
Value
\(\approx 5.391 \times 10^{-44}\ \mathrm{s}\)
Relation
\(t_{\mathrm{P}} = \ell_{\mathrm{P}}/c\)
The smallest interval of time with physical meaning — the time for light to cross \(\ell_{\mathrm{P}}\). The Planck epoch spans \(0 < t < t_{\mathrm{P}}\). All of physics — QFT, GR, thermodynamics — begins only after \(t_{\mathrm{P}}\). The age of the universe today is \(t_0 \approx 4.3 \times 10^{17}\ \mathrm{s} \approx 8\times10^{60}\ t_{\mathrm{P}}\).

The Planck Epoch
Planck epoch
\(0 < t < 10^{-43}\ \mathrm{s}\)
\(T > 10^{32}\ \mathrm{K}\) \(E > 10^{19}\ \mathrm{GeV}\) All 4 forces unified
GR predicts a singularity. QFT requires a fixed spacetime — which doesn't exist here. Spacetime is quantum foam: topology fluctuates wildly. The gravitational coupling \(\alpha_{\rm grav} = (E/E_{\mathrm{P}})^2 = 1\). No confirmed theory describes this epoch.
▾
Grand Unification epoch
\(10^{-43} \to 10^{-36}\ \mathrm{s}\)
\(T \sim 10^{29}–10^{32}\ \mathrm{K}\) \(E \sim 10^{15}–10^{19}\ \mathrm{GeV}\) Gravity separates
Gravity freezes out — the first phase transition in cosmic history. The remaining three forces stay unified at \(E_{\rm GUT} \sim 10^{15}\ \mathrm{GeV}\). Leptogenesis may have generated the baryon asymmetry \(\eta_B \approx 6\times10^{-10}\).
▾
Inflation
\(\sim 10^{-36} \to 10^{-32}\ \mathrm{s}\)
\(a(t) \propto e^{Ht}\) Expansion \(\geq 10^{26}\times\) Seeds LSS
The inflaton \(\phi\) drives exponential expansion. Quantum fluctuations \(\delta\phi_k \sim H/(2\pi)\) become the primordial density perturbations — the seeds of every galaxy and the turnover scale \(k_0\) you are detecting with Euclid and MegaMapper. The primordial spectrum: \(\mathcal{P}_{\mathcal{R}}(k) = A_s(k/k_*)^{n_s-1}\) with \(n_s \approx 0.965\).
▾
Electroweak epoch & reheating
\(10^{-32} \to 10^{-12}\ \mathrm{s}\)
\(T \sim 10^{15}–10^{28}\ \mathrm{K}\) Higgs gets vev Standard Model applies
Inflaton decays, reheating the universe. At \(T_{\rm EW} \approx 160\ \mathrm{GeV}\), the Higgs acquires \(\langle\phi\rangle = v \approx 246\ \mathrm{GeV}\), breaking \(\mathrm{SU}(2)_L\times\mathrm{U}(1)_Y \to \mathrm{U}(1)_{\rm em}\) and giving mass to \(W^\pm\) and \(Z^0\).

QCD Transition & Big Bang Nucleosynthesis
QCD confinement
\(t \sim 10^{-6}\ \mathrm{s}\)
\(T \approx 2\times10^{12}\ \mathrm{K}\) \(\sim 200\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
At \(T \approx 2\times10^{12}\ \mathrm{K}\) (\(\sim 200\ \mathrm{MeV}\)), quarks and gluons confine into protons and neutrons. The quark-gluon plasma gives way to a hadron gas. This transition has been recreated briefly at the LHC and RHIC.
▾
Neutrino decoupling & neutron freeze-out
\(t \approx 1\ \mathrm{s}\)
\(T \approx 10^{10}\ \mathrm{K}\) \(\sim 1\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
Neutrinos decouple from the thermal bath. The neutron-to-proton ratio freezes at \(n/p \approx 1/7\). Free neutrons begin to decay with half-life \(\tau_n \approx 610\ \mathrm{s}\).
▾
Big Bang Nucleosynthesis (BBN)
\(t \approx 1\ \mathrm{s} \to 3\ \mathrm{min}\)
\(Y_p \approx 0.247\) \(\mathrm{D/H} \approx 2.5\times10^{-5}\)
Protons and neutrons fuse to form \({}^2\mathrm{H}\), \({}^3\mathrm{He}\), \({}^4\mathrm{He}\), and \({}^7\mathrm{Li}\). The predicted helium mass fraction \(Y_p \approx 0.247\) matches observations — one of the great triumphs of modern cosmology. Open question: BBN predicts \(\sim 3\times\) more \({}^7\mathrm{Li}\) than observed in metal-poor stars — the lithium problem.
▾

Why Planck Units Matter for LSS Research

Primordial power spectrum

The spectral index \(n_s\) and running \(\alpha_s = dn_s/d\ln k\) encode the physics of the inflaton near \(E_{\mathrm{P}}\). Trans-Planckian effects may produce oscillatory features in \(P(k)\) detectable by next-generation surveys.

Turnover scale \(k_0\)

The turnover at \(k_0 \sim 0.01\text{–}0.02\ h\,\mathrm{Mpc}^{-1}\) encodes \(k_{\rm eq} = a_{\rm eq}H_{\rm eq}\), the Hubble horizon at matter–radiation equality, tracing the thermal history all the way back to the Planck epoch.

Relativistic corrections

Wide-angle effects, Doppler terms, and lensing in the galaxy bispectrum \(B(k_1,k_2,k_3)\) are rooted in the \(1+3\) covariant formalism — a consequence of GR, the very theory that breaks down at \(\ell_{\mathrm{P}}\).

Initial conditions for MCMC

Every MCMC run in cobaya and every Fisher forecast for Euclid assumes \(\mathcal{P}_{\mathcal{R}}(k) \propto k^{n_s-1}\). The Planck epoch is where those initial conditions were set — and where they might deviate from this simplicity.
